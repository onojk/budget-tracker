Section 7 of 7

Plan To Reduce Future Asks

Three levers. Each one changes the monthly math meaningfully. Together, they get us to a position where external help is rarely needed.

Income — restart Uber driving

Within SSDI earned-income limits ($1,620/mo SGA cap). Target $1,000–$1,500/month, starting immediately. At that level we operate at or near break-even without needing additional cuts.

Expenses — audit variable spending

Target $500–$1,000/month in reductions: dining and DoorDash ($300/mo, mostly cuttable), Amazon ($200/mo, can shrink), subscriptions ($80/mo, find unused ones), and grocery discipline — list-based shopping, less prepared food.

Debt — continue aggressive CareCredit paydown

We reduced CareCredit by $1,000 in the last ten days. At that pace, CareCredit clears in under three months, freeing approximately $180/mo currently going to minimum payments.

We expect this to be the largest ask of the year. Each subsequent month should require less. The goal is to reach a place where we ask for help in genuinely lean months — not structurally, every month.
